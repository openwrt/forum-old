I'd then like to have the lan port on the pfsense machine plugged into the lan switchports of the tplink access point which will then serve as an access point with a separate secured network on radio 2 for my own devices, as well as act as a regular switch routing through the pfsense machine for any wired traffic.

I know this must be possible with openwrt but i'm very new and not entirely sure where to start? If anyone has any advice or particular documentation I can reference besides the whole of google and the wiki it'd make things much easier as i've been at it all day and haven't really gotten anywhere. It's a PCI-E card, they are cheap. Get one that works.

That said, if you are willing to spend some $, you can get this, which should work on all the distros.
http://pcengines.ch/apu2b4.htm  About 25% smaller in total volume than your zotac, 3 GB LAN ports, 2 PCI-e card slots, m-sata,  and a quad core that support AES-NI. 

Probably come in around mid twos.  http://pcengines.ch/order1.php?c=4

I am of the opinion that OpenVPN is easier to configure on either pfSense or iPfire.  I was unhappy with the "No USB" policy on pfSence, but in hind sight its not an issue.  I do not remember why I did not use ipFire.  My ALIX was at the very bottom of the specs for both.

As for the archer, this is probably close enough to fill the need, a fraction the price, has OpenWrt installed and only $30.  http://www.gl-inet.com/mt300a/#14425660 … c5095-8cab  Check to see it the TF card reader is installed or not.  In the early ones it was not.  I expect you can plug it in to the USB port on the APU2.
