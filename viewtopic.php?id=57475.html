That unit has 600 mW transmit power and an external antenna port.  According to reviews on Amazon, if your USB port strictly enforces the USB specification's 500 mA limit, it is unlikely to work.  By far the easiest workaround is to use a cheap USB2 powered hub that comes with a plug-in power supply of at least 1000 mA.  I said cheap hub because you do not want any fancy DC current limiting features in it.

Buy something like this:
http://www.amazon.com/AmazonBasics-Port â€¦ +usb+2+hub

Plug the hub's input USB cable into the router's USB data port.  Connect the routers USB power port to one of the sockets on the hub with the A to mini-B cable that came with the router.  Connect the high power wifi adapter to one of the other sockets using the cable that came with it.  Connect the hub's power supply to the hub and the wall.  (The router should be able to get power from the hub, you don't need a separate power supply for it.)

You already have the cables you need, you only need to add the hub and its power supply.  This is all plug and play there is no need for custom wiring.
