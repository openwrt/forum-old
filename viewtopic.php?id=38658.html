Post #4
hus
17 Aug 2012, 11:41

You don't need a second radio for this, unless you want the repeated network to be on a different channel. All hardware I know will happily run a STA and on or more APs on the same phy. The tricky part is bridging (to make the repeater transparent as opposed to creating a new, routed subnet), which is an entirely separate problem.
The primary solution for that would be WDS, which has several different, incompatible implementations - so this depends on the hardware of your main AP. I'm using a bunch of Buffalo WZR-HP-G300NHs (and a WZR-HP-AG300H for 5GHz) for this purpose and I'm very happy with them.

At least in my case, dual WNIC are preferrable by far because:
1. Client WNIC will get a directional antenna, while the master one will use a hi gain omni directional stick
2. Client WNIC will have the maximum TX power, while the other one will not
3. Client WNIC will need to match a specific channel, while it's desirable the master one  goes the "auto" way
